# SRSM V1.0 Release Gate

## Release decision

**BLOCKED**

The current work cannot honestly be promoted to V1.0.

Version numbers do not resolve the outstanding physics. The decisive evidence still shows that the naive nonlinear lattice constraint algebra is anomalous, projected evolution is externally stabilized, the nonlinear degree count bifurcates, and the microscopic relational origin of the geometric variables is not derived.

## Critical correction

The V0.87–V0.99 package contains useful bounded surrogate studies and planning artifacts. It must not be read as thirteen independently completed derivations of the corresponding physical claims. Several later numerical datasets were deliberately illustrative effective-model tests, not outputs from one unified microscopic SRSM implementation.

They can guide engineering, but they cannot satisfy a V1.0 scientific release gate.

## Required V1.0 standard

A defensible V1.0 must be one coherent model with one declared microscopic state space and one action or evolution law. From that same implementation it must demonstrate:

1. exact nonlinear first-class closure;
2. projection-free preservation of all constraints;
3. exactly two local physical configuration degrees;
4. Lorentzian stability and valid reality conditions;
5. a controlled continuum limit;
6. derivation of tetrad, connection, locality, signature, and effective couplings from SRSM relational dynamics;
7. universal matter coupling;
8. recovery of General Relativity in weak-field, wave, cosmological, and at least one strong-field regime;
9. complete reproducibility and explicit falsification gates.

## Immediate executable target: V1.0-RC1

Do not attempt all gates simultaneously.

RC1 should solve the current earliest blocker:

### Exact nonlinear closure from a geometric lattice action

Required implementation:

- SU(2) or Lorentz holonomies on oriented links;
- conjugate fluxes on dual faces;
- the correct holonomy-flux Poisson algebra;
- Gauss constraints generated by vertex gauge transformations;
- plaquette curvature;
- vector and scalar constraints derived from the same discrete action;
- automatic differentiation of the full constraint Jacobian;
- bracket reconstruction against the span of the constraints;
- residual tests on exact or numerically solved constraint-surface configurations;
- lattice-size and perturbation-amplitude convergence.

### RC1 pass condition

For every bracket class,

\[
\{C_A,C_B\}-f_{AB}{}^C C_C
\]

must converge to zero under refinement and must vanish numerically on the solved constraint surface within a declared tolerance that is tighter than the discretization error.

A fitted counterterm or post-step projection is not sufficient.

## Final status

The project is at a serious pre-release research stage. The next honest label is:

**SRSM V1.0-RC1 — nonlinear holonomy-flux closure attempt**

not V1.0.
